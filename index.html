How commonly is the Internet used worldwide?

Computers in this network can communicate in different ways as well as share various types of information. Is there a relationship between Internet user rate and Urban rate / Income per person?

There can be several factors that affect the Internet usage rate. Some factors that are analyzed in this section is the urban rate of a country and the average income per person in a country. It can be seen that the scatterplot on the left shows a correlation between the Internet usage rate and urban rate of a country, indicated by the increasing trend line on the plot. This means that the higher the urban rate of a country, the higher the Internet usage rate.

The scatterplot on the right also shows a correlation between the Internet usage rate and the average income per person which is indicated by the increasing trend line. Generally, this means that the higher the average income, the higher the Internet usage rate in the country. However, there are a few countries that don't correspond to this trend, as shown by the anomaly points on the chart.
